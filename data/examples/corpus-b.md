# Field Report From Planet Serein

Day 14 on Serein was quieter than expected. The silver grass had stopped singing in the wind, which usually meant the temperature would drop before dusk.

Commander Imani sent two scouts toward the basalt ridge while the rest of us repaired the greenhouse dome with resin patches and clipped wire. The tomatoes were doing better than morale. One of them had turned such an unreasonable shade of orange that we named it Deputy Sun.

At 16:20 local time, the western sky split into three pale rings. No alarm sounded, because no one had written a protocol for polite atmospheric miracles. We stood in the dust and watched the light move across the solar panels until every scratched surface looked ceremonial.

Later, in the mess tent, Olek insisted the rings were only ice crystals. Mara insisted they were a welcome signal. I wrote both theories down and added a third: perhaps the planet simply enjoyed being noticed.

If this report reaches Earth, let it show that nothing here feels empty. Strange, yes. Hostile, occasionally. Empty, never.
