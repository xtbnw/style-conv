# Midnight Grocery

Lin Yue opened the corner grocery at six in the morning, even though no one in Glass Harbor bought oranges before sunrise.

He still lined them up in perfect rows because he liked the brief illusion that the town could be organized. By seven, the ferry mechanic arrived for peppermint gum, the florist traded gossip for instant noodles, and a child in a yellow raincoat pressed both hands against the freezer door just to watch the fog bloom.

Nothing dramatic happened on most days. The receipt printer squeaked, the radio forecast changed its mind twice, and the neighbor's old spaniel slept beside the rice sacks like a retired security guard. Lin Yue considered that a good shift.

At noon, a woman he had never seen before bought a notebook, three batteries, and a single peach soda. She asked whether the lighthouse still flashed red on storm nights. Lin Yue told her yes, although everyone in town knew it had been broken for years.

That evening, when he locked the storefront, the lighthouse on the far pier pulsed once in a clean red line across the water.
